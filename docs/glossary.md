# Glossary

- **Attributes** - individual characteristics such as retina, age, address,
citizenship, name
- **Claim** - a statement about identity, such as "my name is Alice" or "I am a
Slovenian citizen"
- **Credential** - documentation such as a driver's license, an ID card, or a
utility bill used to prove identity European Commission
- **Attestation** - third party verification of identity, where someone verifies a
claim, such as when a human at border control validates a passport picture

- **Digital Signature** - is a mathematical scheme for verifying the
authenticity of digital messages or documents. A valid digital signature on a
message gives a recipient confidence that the message came from a sender known
to the recipient.
- **Electronic Signature** - An electronic signature, or e-signature, is data
that is logically associated with other data and which is used by the signatory
to sign the associated data. Electronic signatures are a legal concept distinct
from digital signatures, a cryptographic mechanism often used to implement
electronic signatures.

- **Verifiable Credential** - A tamper-evident credential whose authorship can
be cryptographically verified. Verifiable credentials can be used to build
verifiable presentations, which can also be cryptographically verifiable.

- **Zero Knowledge Proof** - also known as a ZK proof or ZKP is a protocol in
which one party (the prover) can convince another party (the verifier) that some
given statement is true, without conveying to the verifier any information
beyond the mere fact of that statement's truth.
